MGMT's Best Albums

Congratulations

MGMT's Congratulations may initially give the listener whiplash if they expected more of the synth-pop sounds of the band's previous album, Oracular Spectacular. The switch to pure psychedelic rock shows that the duo is willing to dive head first into pure chaos, blending shifting tempos with surreal lyrics that reveals more to the listener on each pass through the tracklist.

Loss of Life

MGMT's most recent album, Loss of Life, takes a much different approach than something like Congratulations. It trades the psychedelic experimentation of their previous works for a much more grounded and melancholy tone. The album focuses on themes of nostalgia and aging, delivered with a constant sense of uneasiness. The power of Loss of Life comes from its subtlety rather than the bombastic sounds of MGMT's previous works.

Little Dark Age

While not as initially well recieved as something like Congratulations, Little Dark Age initially gained immense popularity from the titular single blowing up on social media sites such as TikTok. The album is a return to the synth-pop sounds of MGMT's first album, but with a darker, more sinister feel. While perhaps not as profound as something like Loss of Life, Little Dark Age provides a great entry point into the band's discography without feeling shallow.
